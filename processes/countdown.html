These eleven poems arrive together on October 3.

They are the product of the longest silence I have kept between collections, a silence that was necessary and restless all at once. Some of these words were carried from nights that still ache; others were written to lay those very nights to rest.

The Rehearsed Oracle is not a confession, but a performance that learned, finally, to admit its own exhaustion. I wrote them in fragments, in repetitions, in rehearsals that blurred into prophecy. And now, gathered here, they feel like the record of what remains after the curtain falls.
