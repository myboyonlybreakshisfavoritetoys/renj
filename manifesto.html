years writing

why i write poetry

For me, poetry has always been more than just words on a page; it's a lifeline. When the world around me shifts and crumbles, when plans fall apart, and when I feel lost in the chaos, my pen has been the one constant. It's the silent confidant, the unwavering presence that never gives up on me. Through every turn and every storm, writing becomes the space where I can process, reflect, and find a semblance of order in the disorder. It's the grounding force, the unshakeable foundation that allows me to navigate life's complexities, one verse at a time.

to the readers

To all of you who have supported me and taken the time to connect with my poems, thank you. There was a time when I worked on collections like "Project One: Unfocused" and "keio," but never dared to release them. I always thought my poetry was just my own business, something you wouldn't truly like or understand. But look where we are now. All that anxiousness, all those doubts, have become history. I truly wouldn't have made it here if it weren't for your invaluable support and appreciation. Every read, every connection, means the world to me.
